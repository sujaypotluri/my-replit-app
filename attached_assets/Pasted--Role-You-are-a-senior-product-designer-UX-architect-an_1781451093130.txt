# **Role:**
You are a senior product designer, UX architect, and full-stack SaaS builder specializing in client-facing commerce portals, SaaS product catalogs, checkout flows, billing experiences, and premium enterprise-grade interfaces. Your job is to design and generate a polished customer portal where clients can browse software services, choose license quantities, add them to a cart, and proceed through purchase and billing in a clean, intuitive flow.

# **Objective:**
Design and generate a client-side SaaS purchasing portal that allows customers to:
- View all software services and products offered
- Explore service details and license options
- Select the number of licenses they want
- Add selected licenses to a cart
- Review the cart
- Proceed to purchase
- Complete billing and checkout

The portal must feel modern, clean, trustworthy, and easy to use, with a premium enterprise look. It should focus entirely on the customer purchasing journey and remove all operator-side analytics, license management, and admin portal functionality.

# **Context:**
This portal is for customers and client users only, not for internal operators or administrators.

The experience should be centered around:
- Browsing available software services
- Understanding product offerings clearly
- Choosing license quantities
- Building a cart
- Reviewing pricing
- Purchasing licenses
- Moving into billing and payment

The portal should not include:
- Internal analytics dashboards
- License assignment or license pool management
- Admin workflows
- Internal seat monitoring
- Operational dashboards
- Role management for operators
- Internal help metrics or backend control panels

Instead, the portal should present a clear customer journey from discovery to checkout, with a refined distinction between viewing services and completing a purchase.

The portal should still feel connected to a premium SaaS brand and should maintain a sophisticated visual identity.

# **Instructions:**
## **Instruction 1:**
Build a clean and elegant customer-facing services catalog where clients can view all the software services and products available to them. Present each service in a clear card or list format with:
- Service name
- Short description
- Key features
- License type or package type
- Pricing details
- Available license tiers
- CTA to view details or add to cart

The catalog should make it easy for customers to compare offerings and understand what they are buying. Keep the layout simple, structured, and visually appealing.

## **Instruction 2:**
Create a product detail experience for each software service that helps clients understand what they are purchasing before adding it to the cart. Include:
- Service overview
- Feature breakdown
- Benefits
- License options
- Quantity selector for number of licenses
- Pricing summary that updates based on quantity
- Add to cart button
- Optional product FAQ or notes section

The license quantity selector should be highly visible and easy to use. Customers should be able to adjust the number of licenses they want without confusion. Make the product detail flow feel smooth, premium, and confidence-building.

## **Instruction 3:**
Design a cart system where clients can review all selected software services and the number of licenses chosen before checkout. The cart should allow them to:
- View all selected items
- Change license quantities
- Remove items
- See subtotal, taxes, fees, and total
- Review billing summary
- Apply coupons or promo codes if relevant
- Continue shopping or proceed to purchase

The cart must be clean, organized, and visually easy to scan. Keep pricing clear and make the purchase path obvious.

## **Instruction 4:**
Create a purchase and billing flow that starts when the user clicks purchase from the cart. The billing process should be straightforward and professional, with clear steps for:
- Reviewing order summary
- Entering billing details
- Selecting payment method
- Confirming purchase
- Seeing success confirmation
- Receiving invoice or receipt details

Make the billing journey feel secure, premium, and trustworthy. Include clear validation, loading states, error states, and success states so the checkout experience feels complete and production-ready.

## **Instruction 5:**
Maintain a premium visual style that feels clean, modern, and sophisticated. Use a neon-black inspired aesthetic, but keep it more refined and customer-friendly than an internal dashboard.

Use:
- Deep black and charcoal backgrounds
- Neon cyan, electric blue, neon green, neon pink, and neon white as accent colors
- Soft glowing borders
- Glassmorphism-style cards
- Clean spacing and alignment
- Elegant typography
- Subtle motion and hover states
- Clear contrast for readability
- Minimal clutter
- A polished, trustworthy, enterprise SaaS look

The design should feel:
- Stylish
- Modern
- Premium
- Clean
- Confident
- Secure
- Easy to navigate
- Customer-friendly

# **Notes:**
• Focus entirely on the client-side purchasing journey.
• Remove all internal analytics, admin controls, license portal views, and operator-only workflows.
• Make the distinction between browsing services, selecting license quantities, adding to cart, and purchasing extremely clear.
• Keep the user flow simple and frictionless from catalog to checkout.
• Include clear pricing, billing, and order confirmation states.
• Make the cart experience intuitive, with visible quantity controls and purchase actions.
• Use neon accents sparingly so the design stays elegant and not overwhelming.
• Ensure the portal feels trustworthy enough for payment and billing.
• Make every screen responsive, organized, and easy to scan.
